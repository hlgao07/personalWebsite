My name is Hanlu Gao. Welcome to my indecisive world!

    I am a fashion investigator obsessed with experimental interactive art and installation art currently doing my MFA fashion design program at the Fashion Institute of Technology. I majored in mathematics in my undergraduate year, which distinguishes myself from usual fashion designers.

    My thesis work explores the ambivalent emotion that people have when they doubt concerning two or more possible alternatives or courses of action. I focus on the reasons for causing people indecisive: different perspectives, overloaded choices, and illusions. I am interested in why the hesitant exists in our daily life through research, practical experiments, and multimedia. My work is influenced by my own experience of mathematical bachelor and select phobia.
